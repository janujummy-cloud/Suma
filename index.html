Personal Hygiene

Simple daily habits that keep you healthy and fresh

Wash Hands

Wash hands with soap for at least 20 seconds before eating, after restroom use, and after touching public surfaces. It's the single most effective way to prevent infection.

Clean Environment

Maintain clean surroundings to reduce allergens, prevent pest infestations, and lower the spread of airborne germs. Declutter and disinfect surfaces weekly.

Daily Bath

Daily bathing removes sweat, dead skin cells, and bacteria. Use lukewarm water and mild soap for best results without drying out your skin.

Dental Hygiene

Brush twice daily, floss once, and rinse with mouthwash. Replace your toothbrush every 3 months. Good oral hygiene prevents cavities, gum disease, and bad breath.

Sleep Hygiene

Aim for 7–9 hours per night. Keep a consistent sleep schedule, avoid screens 30 minutes before bed, and keep your bedroom cool and dark.

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses (2 litres) of clean water daily. Hydration supports digestion, skin health, kidney function, and energy levels throughout the day.

Healthy Food

Fuel your body with the right nutrients every day

Nutritious Meals

Eat balanced meals with proteins, complex carbs, healthy fats, and fibre. Fill half your plate with vegetables at every meal.

Fresh Fruits

Fruits provide natural energy, antioxidants, and immune-boosting vitamins. Aim for 2–3 servings daily. Whole fruits are better than juices.

Healthy Salads

Dark leafy greens, colourful veggies, nuts, and seeds make salads nutrient powerhouses. Use olive oil and lemon as dressing.

Limit Processed Food

Cut down on packaged snacks, sugary drinks, and fast food. These are high in sodium, sugar, and unhealthy fats that increase chronic disease risk.

Daily Exercise

Move your body every day for a stronger, happier life

Workout

30 minutes of moderate exercise daily cuts heart disease risk by 35%. Mix cardio with strength training for best results.

Yoga

Reduces cortisol levels, improves flexibility and posture. Even 15 minutes of yoga in the morning sets a calm, focused tone for the day.

Morning Walk

A brisk 20-minute walk improves circulation, elevates mood through endorphin release, and kickstarts metabolism for the day.

Cycling

Low-impact aerobic exercise that builds leg strength, improves cardiovascular health, and is eco-friendly for commuting.
